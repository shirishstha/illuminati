Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) 
            BBA, which stands for Bachelor of Business Administration, is one of the most demanding fields and the first choice for most +2/ intermediate business graduates in Nepal. It is a four-year bachelor's program in business administration divided into 8 semesters with 120 credit hours. This program provides specializations in Banking and Finance, Industrial, Marketing, Travel and Tourism Management, and Management Information Systems. The specialization subjects are chosen by students in the seventh and eighth semesters.

            Objectives of Bachelor of Business Administration
            The BBA program for the students at Tribhuvan University has the following core objectives:
            
            To familiarize real-life business problems and equip them with the ability to understand and solve practical business challenges.
            To prepare for mid-level business positions in various sectors.
            To provide a highly professional education that incorporates information management and computer courses.
            To prepare and motivate them to maximize their potential in post-graduate studies.
            Admission Requirements in Bachelor of Business Administration
            The candidates applying for admission to the Bachelor of Business Administration program at Tribhuvan University must follow the following procedures:
            
            They should have completed their +2 or equivalent level from any university or board recognized by Tribhuvan University.
            They should have secured at least a second division in their +2 or equivalent level.
            They should follow all the application procedures required for the entrance examination.

             
Admission Criteria:

            To gain admission to the Bachelor of Business Administration program at Tribhuvan University, students must pass through various phases of tests:
            
             
Written Test:

            Eligible candidates must appear in the CMAT entrance test (Central Management Aptitude Test) conducted by the Faculty of Management, Tribhuvan University. The test follows international testing standards and consists of four categories: Verbal Skills, Analyzing Quantitative Techniques, Reasoning with Logic, and General Awareness. The test comprises 100 objective questions, with 25 questions in each category. To pass the exam and qualify for an interview, students must score at least 40%.
            
            
Interview:

            Students who score a minimum of 40% on the written test will be called for an interview. A merit list will be prepared, and a few will be selected for admission based on their performance in the interview.
            
            
Entrance Preparation:

            Gaining admission to the top Bachelor of Business Administration colleges at Tribhuvan University is a challenging task that requires effort and hard work. BBA aspirants must take the CMAT entrance exam conducted by Tribhuvan University. Students can either join entrance preparation classes or study at home using CMAT entrance books available at bookstores. Participating in mock tests conducted by institutes can help enhance their capabilities and build confidence. Model questions for the CMAT entrance exam can easily be found on the internet. However, it is generally easier to pass the test if they enroll in a good entrance preparation institute. These institutes not only prepare them for the CMAT test but also provide guidance on facing interviews.
            
            
Career and Scope

            A Bachelor of Business Administration degree helps individuals secure entry-level and mid-level positions in banks, corporations, and various factories. Graduates have the opportunity to gain practical experience and understand the intricacies of business administration by working under and assisting their seniors. They can pursue job positions such as Sales Officers and Management Trainees. BBA provides a solid foundation for those who aspire to pursue an MBA degree in the future. Additionally, Bachelor of Business Administration graduates have a higher chance of finding employment in both governmental and non-governmental sectors.

After BBA:

          
  
key points to consider after completing a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration:



1.Specialized Master's Programs:

Consider pursuing a specialized Master's program such as an MBA (Master of Business Administration) to deepen your knowledge in a specific area like finance, marketing, or entrepreneurship.


2.Professional Certifications:

Obtain relevant professional certifications (e.g., CPA, CFA, PMP) to enhance your skills and credibility in specific business domains.

 
3.Work Experience:

Gain practical work experience through internships or entry-level positions to apply theoretical concepts learned during your undergraduate studies and develop real-world skills.


4.Entrepreneurship and Startups:

Explore entrepreneurial opportunities by starting your own business or joining a startup, applying your business acumen to create and manage ventures.


5.Networking and Professional Development:

Build a strong professional network by attending industry events, joining professional associations, and continuously engaging in professional development to stay current in the business landscape.
