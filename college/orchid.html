Orchid International College

Orchid International College (OIC) is a college in Nepal affiliated with Tribhuvan University (TU). It started in 2010 and was founded by a team of experienced academicians, computer engineers, management professionals, and a respected software company in Nepal. The college aims to offer excellent education, preparing students for the challenges of today's world and laying the groundwork for a successful future.


Scholarships:
Orchid International College (OIC) actively promotes academic excellence by offering scholarships to deserving students who exhibit exceptional academic performance. Students meeting the scholarship criteria are granted 100% scholarships, aiming to alleviate financial barriers and encourage continued educational pursuits.


Computer lab:
The college features a cutting-edge computer lab, providing students with a state-of-the-art facility to practice and improve their IT skills. Accessible during college hours, the lab offers students the flexibility to enhance their technological proficiency at their convenience.


Workshops and seminars:
The college frequently organizes presentations, conferences, seminars, and workshops covering diverse subjects such as IT, computer security, cybercrime, e-governance, HR, organizational behavior, and psychology. Eminent personalities from both the industry and academia are invited to share their expertise and insights during these events.


Library:
OIC's library has a great variety of books and journals in computer science, management, social science, mathematics, and related subjects. Students can borrow books according to their requirements. The library also has audiovisual resources, eBooks, dissertations, annual reports, and case studies for students to access.


Games and Extra Activities:
Orchid International College places a strong emphasis on games and extracurricular activities. Our students actively participate in various events, and They have Their own Futsal ground for playing and practicing cricket and football.
