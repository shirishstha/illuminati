Sainik Awasiya Mahavidyalaya

The Suping Army Campus was established during the tenure of General Singha Pratap Shah, then Chief of Army Staff (COAS). In 1986, the campus was relocated to Sallaghari, Bhaktapur, where it was renamed Birendra Sainik Awasiya Mahavidyalaya after King Birendra Bir Bikram Shah. Since then, the funding of the school has been looked after by Nepal Army Welfare Fund. The institution is controlled administratively by the Nepal Army Headquarters and academically by teachers and professors. The highest officer from the military to administer SAMB is a Lt. Colonel who acts as a liaison officer.

Library: SAMB has well-established libraries for different levels: Junior, Central, +2, and Bachelor’s. These libraries offer abundant textbooks, reference materials, newspapers, periodicals, and CDs. Students are encouraged to borrow books, with reading rooms available. The collection is regularly updated, and library facilities are upgraded. Borrowed books must be returned promptly, with penalties for delays.


sports: Guided by "A healthy mind in a healthy body," SAMB provides a diverse sports program with swimming, football, basketball, volleyball, cricket, table tennis, chess, gymnastics, gym, Taekwondo, and lawn tennis. The three well-maintained playgrounds are overseen by highly experienced national-level coaches. Both day scholars and boarders participate, making SAMB's sports facility unparalleled in the country.


Transportation: The Mahavidyalaya provides shuttle services for day scholar students, ensuring reliability, punctuality, and affordability. Bus routes are arranged to match the convenience of students, subject to necessity and feasibility.


psychological Counselling: The Mahavidyalaya offers psychological counseling for all students, facilitated by trained counselors who are also subject teachers. These dedicated professionals guide and support students with care, helping them overcome challenges and stay on the right path, steering clear of unwanted activities.
