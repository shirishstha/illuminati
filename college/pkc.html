Padma Kanya Campus

Padma Kanya Multiple Campus (PKMC) was founded in 1951 as Nepal's first women's campus (Aswin 2008 BS). Initially, its academic activities were carried out at the current location of Kanya School in Dillibazar. Its academic curriculum began in eight humanities subjects at the time of its founding.


Classrooms: Padma Kanya Campus has more than 50 spacious classrooms with four big halls in Ratna Deep Bhawan and two halls (Sagarmatha hall and Danish hall) in Maitri Bhawan that facilitate seminars, workshop programs.


Hostels: PK Campus offers a new hostel for 200 out-of-town students, providing standard accommodation and affordable hygienic food. The facility enforces strict rules overseen by an experienced lady hostel warden.


Labs There are different separate labs for each subject in Science Stream; they are well-maintained, spacious, and well-equipped. In the faculty of humanities, there are seven different labs with well-equipped â€“ Biochemistry, Dress-Designing, Food and Nutrition, Psychology, Geography, Dance, and Music labs.


Library: Padma Kanya Campus features a dedicated library building with an extensive collection of textbooks, reference materials, research reports, magazines, and newspapers. The campus also houses specialized mini-libraries for Women Studies, Food and Nutrition, and Rural Development. An e-library provides convenient access to online resources.


Scholarships: PK Campus has a policy to offer scholarships and awards to the talented and deserving students. In addition, students from unprivileged section of the society are also considered for freeship. Buddhist Studies- three years bachelor program provides special scholarship to the students on merit basis.
